About Us

The Zeta Beta Mu Fraternity is a brotherhood founded in 1971 at the University of Santo Tomas, rooted in a shared commitment to excellence, leadership, and service among future medical professionals. Guided by its five pillars—Scholarship, Service, Sociability, Sacrifice, and Idealism—the fraternity cultivates well-rounded individuals who strive for academic distinction, meaningful community engagement, strong bonds of brotherhood, selfless dedication, and principled living. Through generations, it has continued to uphold its legacy of shaping competent, compassionate physicians and leaders dedicated to making a lasting impact in society.

The Five Pillars

The values that have guided every brother of Zeta Beta Mu since 1971.

Scholarship

A relentless pursuit of academic excellence and lifelong learning, fostering intellectual growth and distinction in medicine.

Service

Giving back through medical missions, free clinics, and community work — caring for those who need us most.

Sociability

A genuine brotherhood and fellowship that connects members across generations, hospitals, and borders for life.

Sacrifice

Selfless dedication to our patients, our brothers, and our country — putting service above self.

Idealism

Upholding the highest ethical and moral standards, serving as principled role models for future generations.

Service in Action

Supporting Villa Maria Integrated School

In the spirit of our pillar of Service, Zeta Beta Mu proudly supports Villa Maria Integrated School in Porac, Pampanga — a school dedicated to Aeta students. Through gift-giving drives, educational supplies, and medical outreach, our brothers help nurture the next generation of this community.
